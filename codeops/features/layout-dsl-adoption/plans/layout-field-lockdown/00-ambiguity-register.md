Every decision below was put to the maintainer with grounded options and decided by them. Rows
marked *(measured)* were settled by evidence gathered in a reverted spike — the field was flipped
to `readonly`, `ui` was rebuilt so downstream packages saw the new `.d.ts`, and `tsc --noEmit` ran
per package. The tree was restored and `dist` rebuilt afterwards.

| AR-1 | Design *(measured)* | How deep the lockdown goes. ## Latent-defect policy (from AR-5's neighbourhood, decided with it)

Three of #132's errors sit in test files that **currently pass** — 4× `TS2722` *"cannot invoke an
object which is possibly undefined"* in `test/datagrid-showcase.spy-source.spec.test.ts`, a
`TS2554` arity error in `test/recipes.smoke.spec.test.ts`, a `TS2345` wheel-event shape mismatch in
`test/probe-readout.impl.test.ts`, and 2× `TS2740` stream-type mismatches in
`test/probe-nontty.spec.test.ts`.

Preflight added two more, in `docs-site` spec oracles that likewise pass today:
`docs-site/test/demo-shell.spec.test.ts:82` (`TS2345` — `Application` not assignable to
`{ desktop: { children: readonly View[] } }`) and `docs-site/test/example-at.spec.test.ts:84`
(`TS2322` — `Application | View` not assignable to `View`).

**Decision: investigate and fix each properly; do not silence them.** A type error in a passing
test means either the fixture is wrong or the assertion is weaker than it reads — and these are
spec oracles, where a test that passes by accident is worse than no test. Each gets a one-line
verdict recorded during execution. Minimal non-null assertions were rejected: they convert a
signal into silence in exactly the files meant to be the oracle.

## Gate statement

✅ **GATE PASSED (re-passed after preflight)** — 16 items, 16 resolved, 0 deferred. Every scope, design, sequencing and
verification decision in this plan traces to a row above. AR-13 and AR-14 were raised *during* planning, when a write-site inventory revealed the test surface, and were decided before any further document was written (the surface-during-authoring rule). AR-1 through AR-4 were decided against
measured evidence from the reverted spike rather than from reasoning about TypeScript's semantics;
that distinction matters, because the naive reading of `readonly` produced a **21-site** worklist
against a true surface of **114**.

**What preflight changed, and the lesson in it.** The spike measured the *write sites* correctly and
the *blast radius* incompletely. Five corrections came out of it — AR-2's holder survey was scoped to
`src/` but stated repo-wide (and a committed impl test asserts the opposite of the new contract);
AR-4 generalized the invalidate pairing from three sites to all thirty-two; AR-5 counted 8 `.mjs`
imports where 11 exist; AR-11 declared "zero coupling" to a ratchet the flip demonstrably trips; and
AR-13's baseline missed two packages. The pattern is consistent and worth naming: **the spike asked
"what stops compiling?" and never asked "what stops *passing*?"** — the `@example` guard, the plugin
API-ref snapshot, the identity assertions and the documentation snippets are all inside `yarn verify`
and none of them are type errors. AC-9 now names them.
